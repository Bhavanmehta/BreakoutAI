What's working, what's not

A plain-English diagnostic of BreakoutAI's stock-picking engine — based on a 2+ year / ~293,000-event backtest plus the actual live picks from the last 2 weeks (Jul 2–19, 2026).

TL;DR

  • The engine scans stocks with 36 different detection strategies ("methods") — things like "relative strength," "volume squeeze," "high conviction combo," etc. Each one fires a "buy signal" independently.
  • Averaged across everything, a signal is right about 41% of the time in India and 36% of the time in the US — that's the baseline to beat.
  • A handful of methods are reliably better than that baseline in both markets (relative-strength, "high conviction" combos, retuned pre-breakout). A handful are reliably worse in both markets — most notably one very high-volume method (A_donchian_minervini) and everything built on top of it.
  • Two of the engine's own "smart" filters are quietly backwards: stocks with the strongest measured relative strength and the biggest volume spikes actually do worse, not better, in the data.
  • Real live results so far: India 52.0% win rate (running hotter than the 41.1% backtest baseline, on 273 resolved trades), US 32.5% (running colder than the 36.0% backtest baseline, on 265 resolved trades) — both samples are now large enough to take seriously.
  • Bottom section has a proposed "gate" — which signals to lean on more, and which to mute or require confirmation from another signal before acting on.

2 · How a Pick Gets Made (Plain English)

Every day, the system runs 36 different "detectors" over every stock in the universe (~1,800 in India, ~4,200 in the US). Each detector is a different theory about what makes a stock about to break out. When a stock trips a detector, that's a "method" firing. Sometimes several detectors fire on the same stock the same day — that's called a "combo."

E_relative_strength / E2_...

Stock is outperforming the broader market. The most-used signal by far, and one of the more reliable ones.

A_donchian_minervini

Classic breakout-above-recent-range pattern. Huge volume of signals, but underperforms the average in both markets.

G2_pre_breakout_retuned

A recalibrated "about to break out" detector — consistently one of the better performers.

HC_tier1_high_conviction

The system's own top-confidence bucket. Smaller volume of picks, but a real, consistent edge.

AE / AI / AJ_combo

Donchian-Minervini stacked with other signals. Inherits the weakness — combos don't fix a weak base signal.

M_shakeout_rebreak

Stock got shaken out of a range then re-broke it. Sounds smart, performs below average in both markets.

Conviction score

A 0–100 confidence number the system assigns each pick, blending several of the above.

"Hit rate"

% of the time a signal's picks eventually hit their target price before their stop-loss.

4 · Three Filters That Are Quietly Backwards

These aren't "methods" — they're the underlying scoring criteria the system uses to rank picks. The data shows the opposite of what the design intended.

🔻 "Strong relative strength" picks actually do worse

Stocks are bucketed into quartiles by how strong their relative-strength reading is. In India, the weakest-RS quartile hits 46.5% of the time, while the strongest-RS quartile hits only 36.3%. The stronger the reading, the worse the outcome — almost certainly because those stocks are already extended / everyone's already bought them by the time the signal fires.

India: Q1(weak)=46.5% → Q4(strong)=36.3%  |  a straight-line decline across all 4 quartiles
🔻 Big volume spikes are a red flag, not confirmation, in the US

The system treats a volume surge as a bullish confirmation. In the US, it's the opposite: low-volume days hit 39.5%, but the highest volume-surge quartile hits only 32.3%. Chasing the loudest, most obvious volume spikes is picking up the crowd that's already too late.

US: Q1(low vol)=39.5% → Q4(high surge)=32.3%
🔻 "Deep base" patterns underperform shallow ones (US)

Conventional technical-analysis wisdom says a deeper pullback before breakout ("deep base") sets up a bigger move. In the US data it's reversed: shallow bases hit 40.1%, deep bases hit only 30.4%.

US: Q1(shallow)=40.1% → Q4(deep)=30.4%
🟡 Labeled chart patterns don't add value — "no clear pattern" does just as well or better

The system tags each setup with a chart pattern (Cup & Handle, Ascending Triangle, etc). In both markets, stocks with no identifiable pattern perform at or near the top, while "Ascending Triangle" and "Tight Consolidation" are the worst-performing labels. The pattern-recognition feature isn't currently adding predictive value.

India: No pattern=43.1% (best) vs Ascending Triangle=33.8% (worst)  |  US: No pattern=38.8% vs Ascending Triangle=28.7% (worst)

🔧 Filter logic to fix separately

Reverse or drop the "strong RS ratio" and "high volume surge" scoring boosts (they're currently rewarding the two worst-performing quartiles). Consider removing the chart-pattern label from the conviction score entirely — it's not adding signal.
