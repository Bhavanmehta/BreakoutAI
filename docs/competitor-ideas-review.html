⚖️ IP & licensing ground rules (read once)

The safe principle: copyright protects expression (their exact code, wording, CSS, layouts, logos) and trademark protects names — but ideas, features, methods and public facts are free to use. So we reimplement concepts in our own code, our own words, our own visual design, and we rename anything brand-flavoured.

Do NOT reuse: their product/feature names (GEM score, Cairn, Hopper Feeder, tier words Gem/Bounce/Coal), their exact marketing copy, their tagline, their CSS/layout pixel-for-pixel, their logos, or any scraped scores/report text.

Free to use outright: public academic formulas — Beneish M‑score (1999), Altman Z‑score (1968), DCF/EPV, RSS math, beta/volume screens — plus generic UX patterns (two-column pros/cons, score rings, post-mortems). These are not theirs.

IP Clear — generic concept / public formula Rename — swap their term for ours Design-original — build our own visuals, don't copy layout
