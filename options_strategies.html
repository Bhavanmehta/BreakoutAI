ⓘ Why is max profit tiny but max loss huge? (tap to expand — important)

All four strategies sell option premium with a hedge — the market equivalent of selling insurance. You collect a small credit that you keep most weeks, and occasionally pay out a lot in a bad week. Example: sell the 23,900 PE at 12.25, buy the 23,700 PE at 3.90 → you pocket 8.35 points (× lot = the max profit). The strikes are 200 points apart, so the worst case is 200 − 8.35 points against you. That asymmetry is structural, not a bug.

Two things make it survivable: ① win rate — these setups expired worthless 78–95% of the time in our backtest (shown on each card), and ② the stop-loss — most variants exit at a fixed multiple of the credit, so the typical loss is close to the stop, not the theoretical max. Max loss only happens if price gaps straight through the stop overnight. Judge each card by win rate × typical win vs typical loss, not by the scary max-loss number alone — but never sell a spread whose max loss you can't afford.
